Device Best for: Where to purchase

Front-wheeled walker

How to use a walker
  • People who are very unsteady on their feet and/or fall frequently
  • People who are not able to or are not allowed to put full weight on one leg
  • Amazon
  • Bellevue Healthcare
  • Walgreens

Four-wheeled Walker (Rollator)

How to use four-wheeled walker
  • People who are a little bit unsteady on their feet
  • People who need frequent seated rest breaks due to poor endurance or respiratory issues
  • Caution: four-wheeled walkers are not for people who cannot put full weight on one leg
  • Caution: four-wheeled walkers cannot be safely used as wheelchairs
  • Amazon
  • Bellevue Healthcare
  • Walgreens

Single-Point Cane

How to use a single-point cane
  • People who feel a little bit unsteady on their feet but do not fall frequently
  • Caution: canes cannot be used by people who are not allowed by their doctor to put full weight on one leg
  • Amazon
  • Bellevue Healthcare
  • Walgreens

Quad Cane

How to use a quad cane
  • For people who are unsteady on their feet and fall frequently but who cannot put both hands on a walker (e.g. stroke, fracture)
  • Caution: quad canes cannot be used by people who are not allowed by their doctor to put full weight on one leg
  • Amazon
  • Bellevue Healthcare
  • Walgreens

Wheelchair

How to use a wheelchair
  • People who are extremely unsteady on their feet
  • People who cannot walk the distances that they need to (with or without help)
  • People who must keep the weight off of one or more limbs but who are unable to do so when using a walker
  • Bellevue Healthcare
  • Walgreens
  • Apria Direct
